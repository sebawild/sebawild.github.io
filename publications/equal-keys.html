Sorting Discrete i.i.d. Inputs: Quicksort is Optimal

Aug 2016 (Written: Jul 2016)

Sebastian Wild:

arXiv

| arxiv | slides |

In this paper, I give the first analysis of Quicksort with median-of-$k$ pivot sampling on inputs with equal elements. The result settles a long-standing open problem, the Sedgewick-Bentley conjecture, posed in two talks by Robert Sedgewick:

Median-of-$k$ Quicksort with fat-pivot partitioning has asymptotically optimal average behavior on any randomly ordered input up to a constant factor, and that factor converges to 1 as $k\to\infty$.

(New research on the theory and practice of sorting and Quicksort is Optimal)

Fat-pivot partitioning means that we split the input into three segments: elements strictly smaller than the pivot, strictly larger than the pivot, and equal to the pivot. Hence all duplicates of the pivot are removed from recursive calls.

Input Model

Previous work focused on random permutations of multisets, but little progress has been made in that model since Sedgewick’s seminal 1977 paper. I consider instead $n$ elements chosen i.i.d. (independently and identically distributed) according to a given discrete distribution with probability weights $\mathbf q = (q_1,\ldots,q_u)$.

General Case for Discrete Uniform Inputs

The key ingredient for the analysis, the separation theorem that expresses Quicksort’s cost as $a_{\mathbf q} n \pm O(n^{1-\varepsilon})$, is already given in my dissertation (Theorem 8.16) for the general case of $s$-way Quicksort with generic one-pass partitioning.

In that general setting, an explicit expression for $a_{\mathbf q}$ is still to be found. For the special case of a discrete uniform distribution in $ \lbrace 1,\ldots,u \rbrace $, I found a general asymptotic solution (Theorem 8.17); technicalities aside, the result is as for random permutations with $\ln(n)$ replaced by $\ln(u)$.

I presented this part of the analysis in Dagstuhl, see the slides linked above.

Single-Pivot Quicksort on Discrete i.i.d. Inputs

The arxiv paper gives $a_{\mathbf q}$ for median-of-$k$ Quicksort on $n$ elements i.i.d. from any discrete distribution $\mathbf q$, as long as the universe size $u$ is much smaller than $n$. The exact assumptions are a bit of a nuisance and can probably be improved.
